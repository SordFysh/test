Assembling Your Skycase

Follow our easy step-by-step guide on how to build your new Skycase!

Download Assembly Instructions (PDF)



{{stepTitle[0]}}

Parts List:

  • 4-40 nuts (x4)
  • 4-40 screws (x4)
  • Small accent spacer (x1)
  • Large accent spacer (x1)
  • Base (x1)
  • M3x20mm standoffs (x4)
  • M3x10mm screws (x8)
  • L spacer (x2)
  • Long spacer bar (x1)
  • Short spacer bar (x4)
  • Top accent (x1)
  • Top (x1)

Tools Required:

  • Phillips #2 screw driver

{{stepTitle[1]}}

Remove protective paper from all parts.

Note: if paper is difficult to remove, try pressing your fingernail into the edge of the paper rather than pulling. It will shift the paper over, making it easier to then peel off

{{stepTitle[2]}}

Build base with Raspberry Pi. Make sure the Pi is aligned as shown with the USB ports on the opposite end of the side of the base with the indent.

Use the four 4-40 screws and the four 4-40 nuts. These screws and nuts are non-conductive.

{{stepTitle[3]}}

Insert M3 screws from bottom into M3x20 standoffs. Then start adding spacers as shown.

{{stepTitle[4]}}

Add the rest of the spacers, accent piece, and top.

Plug in any inputs that have ports located on top of the Pi. We suggest threading cords and ribbons, such as the camera ribbon, through the skycase logo on the top piece before securing it.

Secure top with 4 M3 screws.
